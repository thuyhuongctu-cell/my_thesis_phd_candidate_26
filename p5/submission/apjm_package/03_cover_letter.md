**Submission of Manuscript: "The Export Intensity–Performance Relationship in Chinese Private Firms: A Threshold-Stability Perspective"**

Dear Editor-in-Chief,

We are pleased to submit the above manuscript for consideration for publication in the *Asia Pacific Journal of Management* (APJM). The manuscript examines the export intensity–performance relationship across two waves of World Bank Enterprise Survey data for Chinese private firms (2012 and 2024), focusing on the stability of nonlinear turning points and the moderating roles of technological capability and digital adoption.

**Relevance to APJM**

The study is a strong fit for APJM's focus on management and organisation in the Asia-Pacific region, and particularly its deep coverage of Chinese firm strategy. China's private sector is one of the largest and most institutionally complex contexts for internationalisation–performance research; our cross-wave design tests whether the curvilinear relationship is structurally durable or wave-specific across a decade of China's institutional and digital transition, addressing a question of direct interest to APJM's readership and its institution-based-view tradition.

**Key Contributions**

First, a robust inverted-U relationship is confirmed in both waves, with turning points in a tight band (49.4% in 2012, 47.2% in 2024, 48.8% pooled). Paternoster (1998) tests do not reject coefficient equality across waves (FSTS z = +0.82, p = .412), evidencing the structural durability of the coordination-cost mechanism. Second, technological capability is robustly and positively associated with labour productivity in both waves, with returns rising over the period, consistent with a resource-based reading. Third, foundational digital adoption is positive but retained as a baseline control, consistent with a selection-dominated reading in an economy with widespread internet diffusion but heterogeneous foundational infrastructure.
