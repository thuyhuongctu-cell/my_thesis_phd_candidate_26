# International Business Theories - Reference Guide

Tài liệu này cung cấp overview về các lý thuyết chính trong International Business research để hỗ trợ việc xây dựng theoretical foundation cho conceptual framework.

## Transaction Cost Theory (TCT)

**Core Logic:**
Firms choose governance structures (market, hierarchy, hybrid) that minimize transaction costs.

**Key Constructs:**
- Asset specificity: Độ đặc thù của tài sản
- Uncertainty: Không chắc chắn về môi trường, đối tác
- Frequency: Tần suất giao dịch
- Opportunism: Hành vi cơ hội của đối tác

**Khi nào dùng trong IB:**
- Entry mode choice (export vs. FDI vs. licensing)
- Location decisions (offshore vs. nearshore)
- Governance of international operations
- Make-or-buy decisions in global value chains

**Typical Relationships:**
- High asset specificity → Prefer internalization (FDI, wholly-owned subsidiary)
- High uncertainty → Prefer flexible governance (joint ventures, alliances)
- High opportunism risk → Prefer hierarchical control

**Example Framework Application:**
"We apply TCT to explain entry mode choice. When asset specificity is high (e.g., proprietary technology), firms prefer wholly-owned subsidiaries to protect against opportunism. Conversely, when uncertainty is high, joint ventures offer flexibility and local knowledge."

## Resource-Based View (RBV)

**Core Logic:**
Firm competitive advantage stems from valuable, rare, inimitable, and non-substitutable (VRIN) resources.

**Key Constructs:**
- Tangible resources: Physical assets, financial capital
- Intangible resources: Brand, reputation, knowledge
- Capabilities: Organizational routines, processes
- Core competencies: Unique combinations of resources

**Khi nào dùng trong IB:**
- Explaining international competitive advantage
- Foreign market entry and expansion
- Knowledge transfer across borders
- Subsidiary capabilities and performance

**Typical Relationships:**
- Unique capabilities → Successful internationalization
- Resource heterogeneity → Performance differences in foreign markets
- Capability development → Sustained competitive advantage

**Example Framework Application:**
"Drawing on RBV, we argue that firms with superior digital capabilities (a VRIN resource) achieve faster internationalization. These capabilities enable efficient knowledge acquisition and market sensing, creating competitive advantage in foreign markets."

## Institutional Theory

**Core Logic:**
Firms seek legitimacy by conforming to institutional pressures (regulative, normative, cognitive).

**Key Constructs:**
- Institutional distance: Differences in formal and informal institutions
- Isomorphism: Mimetic, coercive, normative pressures
- Legitimacy: Social acceptance and conformity
- Institutional voids: Absence of market-supporting institutions

**Khi nào dùng trong IB:**
- Entry into emerging markets
- Institutional distance effects on performance
- Legitimacy-seeking strategies
- Emerging market MNEs (EM-MNEs) going global

**Typical Relationships:**
- Greater institutional distance → Higher entry barriers, adaptation needs
- Institutional voids → Preference for network-based strategies
- Legitimacy deficits → Need for signaling and conformity

**Example Framework Application:**
"We employ institutional theory to examine how institutional distance moderates the entry mode-performance relationship. Greater institutional distance increases liability of foreignness, making joint ventures with local partners more effective than wholly-owned subsidiaries."

## Uppsala Internationalization Model

**Core Logic:**
Internationalization is a gradual, learning-driven process. Firms enter psychically close markets first, then expand incrementally.

**Key Constructs:**
- Psychic distance: Perceived differences in culture, language, business practices
- Experiential knowledge: Learning from foreign operations
- Commitment: Resource investment in foreign markets
- Incremental stages: Export → Sales subsidiary → Production subsidiary

**Khi nào dùng trong IB:**
- SME internationalization patterns
- Sequential market entry
- Learning and knowledge accumulation
- Contrasting with born globals

**Typical Relationships:**
- Greater psychic distance → Slower, more cautious entry
- Experiential knowledge → Increased commitment
- Incremental learning → Gradual expansion

**Example Framework Application:**
"Consistent with Uppsala model, we propose that SMEs with limited international experience enter psychically close markets first. As experiential knowledge accumulates, they progressively enter more distant markets and increase commitment levels."

## Network Theory

**Core Logic:**
Internationalization is driven by network relationships. Firms leverage ties with customers, suppliers, partners, and institutions.

**Key Constructs:**
- Network ties: Strong vs. weak ties
- Network position: Centrality, brokerage
- Social capital: Trust, reciprocity, shared norms
- Network learning: Knowledge flows through relationships

**Khi nào dùng trong IB:**
- Born globals and rapid internationalization
- SME internationalization via networks
- Alliance formation and management
- Diaspora networks and ethnic entrepreneurship

**Typical Relationships:**
- Strong network ties → Access to foreign market knowledge
- Network centrality → Greater internationalization opportunities
- Social capital → Reduced transaction costs, faster entry

**Example Framework Application:**
"We apply network theory to explain born global emergence. Entrepreneurs with extensive international networks gain early access to foreign market knowledge and opportunities, enabling rapid internationalization despite resource constraints."

## OLI Framework (Eclectic Paradigm)

**Core Logic:**
FDI occurs when firms possess Ownership, Location, and Internalization advantages.

**Key Constructs:**
- Ownership advantages (O): Firm-specific assets (technology, brand, management)
- Location advantages (L): Host country attractiveness (resources, market size, infrastructure)
- Internalization advantages (I): Benefits of internal governance over market transactions

**Khi nào dùng trong IB:**
- FDI decisions and location choice
- MNE strategy and structure
- Comparing different entry modes

**Typical Relationships:**
- O + L + I present → FDI preferred
- O + L but no I → Licensing or exporting preferred
- Weak L → Avoid that location

**Example Framework Application:**
"Using the OLI framework, we examine location choice for R&D subsidiaries. Firms with strong O advantages (proprietary technology) select locations with L advantages (skilled labor, innovation infrastructure) and internalize (I advantage) to protect intellectual property."

## Knowledge-Based View (KBV)

**Core Logic:**
Knowledge is the most strategic resource. Firms exist to create, transfer, and apply knowledge.

**Key Constructs:**
- Tacit vs. explicit knowledge
- Knowledge transfer mechanisms
- Absorptive capacity: Ability to recognize, assimilate, and apply external knowledge
- Knowledge integration

**Khi nào dùng trong IB:**
- Knowledge transfer in MNEs
- Subsidiary roles (knowledge sources vs. receivers)
- Learning from foreign operations
- Absorptive capacity in internationalization

**Typical Relationships:**
- Absorptive capacity → Effective knowledge transfer
- Tacit knowledge → Difficult to transfer, requires direct interaction
- Knowledge integration capabilities → Innovation and performance

**Example Framework Application:**
"Drawing on KBV, we propose that absorptive capacity moderates the relationship between foreign market knowledge and internationalization speed. Firms with higher absorptive capacity can more effectively assimilate and apply external knowledge, accelerating entry."

## Integrating Multiple Theories

Nhiều nghiên cứu IB xuất sắc integrate multiple theories để giải thích phenomena phức tạp:

**Example 1: TCT + RBV for Entry Mode**
- RBV explains WHAT resources drive internationalization
- TCT explains HOW to govern those resources (entry mode choice)

**Example 2: Institutional Theory + Network Theory for EM-MNEs**
- Institutional theory explains legitimacy challenges in developed markets
- Network theory explains how EM-MNEs overcome these via network ties

**Example 3: Uppsala + Network Theory for Born Globals**
- Uppsala explains traditional gradual internationalization
- Network theory explains deviation: born globals leverage networks to leapfrog stages

**Khi integrate theories:**
- Clarify the role of each theory (complementary, contradictory, boundary conditions)
- Explain how they jointly contribute to understanding the phenomenon
- Avoid superficial name-dropping; show genuine theoretical integration

## Chọn Lý Thuyết Phù Hợp

**Bước 1:** Xác định core phenomenon
- Entry mode choice → TCT, OLI
- Competitive advantage → RBV, KBV
- Legitimacy, adaptation → Institutional Theory
- Gradual learning → Uppsala Model
- Rapid internationalization → Network Theory

**Bước 2:** Xem xét context
- Developed markets → TCT, RBV thường đủ
- Emerging markets → Institutional Theory critical
- SMEs, entrepreneurs → Network Theory, Uppsala
- MNEs, large firms → OLI, RBV, KBV

**Bước 3:** Identify gaps
- Existing theories không fully explain phenomenon?
- Cần integrate multiple perspectives?
- Có boundary conditions (moderators) từ theories khác?

**Lưu ý:** Không có "one best theory." Chọn theory dựa trên research question, context, và explanatory power. Tốt nhất là demonstrate deep understanding of 1-2 theories rather than superficially citing 5-6 theories.

**TCT:**
- Williamson, O. E. (1975). Markets and Hierarchies.
- Hennart, J. F. (1982). A Theory of Multinational Enterprise.

**RBV:**
- Barney, J. (1991). Firm resources and sustained competitive advantage. JOM.
- Peteraf, M. A. (1993). The cornerstones of competitive advantage. SMJ.

**Institutional Theory:**
- DiMaggio & Powell (1983). The iron cage revisited. ASR.
- Kostova & Zaheer (1999). Organizational legitimacy under conditions of complexity. AMR.

**Uppsala Model:**
- Johanson & Vahlne (1977). The internationalization process of the firm. JIBS.
- Johanson & Vahlne (2009). The Uppsala internationalization process model revisited. JIBS.

**Network Theory:**
- Johanson & Mattsson (1988). Internationalization in industrial systems. In Strategies in Global Competition.
- Coviello & Munro (1997). Network relationships and the internationalization process of small software firms. IBR.

**OLI:**
- Dunning, J. H. (1980). Toward an eclectic theory of international production. JIBS.
- Dunning, J. H. (2000). The eclectic paradigm as an envelope for economic and business theories of MNE activity. IBR.

**KBV:**
- Grant, R. M. (1996). Toward a knowledge-based theory of the firm. SMJ.
- Cohen & Levinthal (1990). Absorptive capacity. ASQ.

Những papers này là must-read nếu bạn muốn build rigorous theoretical foundation cho conceptual framework trong IB research.
