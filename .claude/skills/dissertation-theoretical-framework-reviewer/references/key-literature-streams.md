# Key Literature Streams for Internationalization-Performance Research

This reference provides an overview of the major literature streams that inform internationalization-performance (I-P) research, with focus on recent developments (2019-2026) and emerging market contexts.

## 1. Meta-Analyses on I-P Relationship

### Foundational Meta-Analyses

**Bausch & Krist (2007)**
- First comprehensive meta-analysis with 92 samples
- Found positive but small effect (r=0.156)
- Highlighted high heterogeneity across studies
- Implication: Single-theory explanations insufficient

**Kirca et al. (2012)**
- Examined moderators of I-P relationship
- Found industry, firm size, and internationalization measure matter
- Emphasized need for contingency perspectives

**Marano et al. (2016)**
- Focused on institutional moderators
- Showed home-country institutions significantly moderate I-P
- Foundational for institutional-layer theorizing

**Wu et al. (2022)** — Most Recent
- Twenty-year meta-analysis of emerging market MNEs
- 218 effect sizes from 186 studies (1998-2021)
- Key findings:
  - Overall positive I-P relationship in EMNEs
  - Home-country government quality positively moderates
  - Nationalism negatively moderates
  - Significant heterogeneity across regions
- Critical for emerging market contextualization

**Arte & Larimo (2022)**
- Examined performance effects of internationalization strategies
- Distinguished between entry modes
- Relevant for mechanism specification

### Implications for Your Framework

- Your multi-theory integration addresses the heterogeneity problem identified in meta-analyses
- Asian context fills gap (underrepresented in prior meta-analyses)
- Need to position your expected effect sizes relative to meta-analytic benchmarks

## 2. Digital Internationalization Stream

### Theoretical Foundations

**Banalieva & Dhanaraj (2019)** — Critical
- "Internalization theory for the digital economy"
- Argues traditional internalization logic needs updating
- Key concepts:
  - Data as new strategic asset
  - Platform-based governance
  - Reduced need for physical presence
- Directly supports your digital lens extension

**Stallkamp & Schotter (2021)**
- Platform firms internationalize differently
- Network effects transcend borders
- Challenges Uppsala sequential logic
- Relevant for your Uppsala re-positioning

**Verhoef et al. (2021)** — Framework Paper
- Three-stage digital transformation:
  1. Digitization (analog → digital data)
  2. Digitalization (process automation)
  3. Digital transformation (business model change)
- Your DAI vs. TCI distinction aligns with this framework

**Yang et al. (2025)** — Most Recent
- Systematic review of digital firm internationalization
- Identifies gaps in understanding digital-traditional firm differences
- Your study contributes to this emerging stream

### Empirical Evidence

**Bhandari et al. (2023)**
- Resource orchestration in digital age
- Digital capabilities as dynamic capabilities
- Supports your RBV extension

**Bustamante et al. (2022)**
- Digital capabilities in SME internationalization
- Emerging market context
- Methodologically relevant for your DAI operationalization

**Chen & Meng (2022)**
- Digital technology and export performance
- Chinese firm evidence
- Relevant for your China two-wave analysis

### Implications for Your Framework

- Digital lens is not just "add-on" but theoretically grounded extension
- Distinguish clearly between capability depth (TCI) and adoption breadth (DAI)
- Position your work as bridging traditional I-P research and digital transformation literature

## 3. Institutional Theory in IB

### Core Institutional Works

**North (1990)** — Foundational
- Institutions as "rules of the game"
- Formal vs. informal institutions
- Transaction cost implications

**Scott (1995)**
- Three pillars: regulative, normative, cognitive
- Multi-dimensional view of institutions

**Khanna & Palepu (2010)** — Institutional Voids
- Concept of institutional voids in emerging markets
- Intermediary institutions missing
- Directly supports your ICRV framework

### Emerging Market Institutional Research

**Peng (2003)**
- Institution-based view of strategy
- Institutions as third leg (beyond industry and resources)
- Foundational for your institutional layer

**Cuervo-Cazurra et al. (2018)**
- Home-country uncertainty as capability builder
- EMNEs learn to manage uncertainty
- Relevant for your "institutional obstacles" hypothesis

**Marano et al. (2016)** — Meta-Analytic Evidence
- Home-country institutions moderate I-P
- Effect stronger in emerging markets
- Directly supports your H5

### Governance Quality Measures

**Kaufmann et al. (2011)** — WGI
- Worldwide Governance Indicators
- Six dimensions of governance quality
- Basis for your ICRV regime classification

### Implications for Your Framework

- ICRV framework operationalizes institutional theory for multi-country Asian context
- Six-regime classification captures institutional gradient
- Digital shield hypothesis (H5) is novel contribution linking digital and institutional layers

## 4. Resource-Based View & Dynamic Capabilities

### Core RBV Works

**Wernerfelt (1984)** — Origin
- Resource-based view foundation
- Firm as bundle of resources

**Barney (1991)** — VRIN Framework
- Valuable, Rare, Inimitable, Non-substitutable
- Source of sustained competitive advantage
- Basis for your TCI logic

**Teece et al. (1997)** — Dynamic Capabilities
- Sense, seize, reconfigure
- Adaptation to changing environments
- Links RBV to internationalization context

### RBV in Internationalization

**Hitt et al. (2006)**
- Resource complementarity in international strategy
- Technological resources enable international expansion
- Supports your H2

**Peng (2001)**
- Resource-based view in emerging economies
- Institutional and resource perspectives interact
- Relevant for your RBV-Institution integration

### Digital Capabilities as Resources

**Bharadwaj (2000)** — IT Resources
- IT resources as strategic assets
- Foundation for digital capability research

**Recent work (2020-2025)**:
- Digital capabilities as VRIN resources
- Particularly valuable in emerging markets with institutional voids
- Your TCI and DAI distinction advances this stream

### Implications for Your Framework

- RBV provides micro-foundation for firm heterogeneity in I-P relationship
- TCI as deep capability vs. DAI as surface adoption is conceptual contribution
- Links to dynamic capabilities literature through adaptation mechanism

## 5. Uppsala Model & Process Perspectives

### Original Uppsala Works

**Johanson & Vahlne (1977)** — Original Model
- Incremental internationalization
- Psychic distance and establishment chain
- Learning-by-doing emphasis

**Johanson & Vahlne (2009)** — Revised Model
- Network perspective added
- Liability of outsidership
- Business relationships central

### Critiques and Extensions

**Lu & Beamish (2004)**
- S-curve hypothesis
- Three-stage internationalization
- Supports your H1 curvilinear logic

**Contractor et al. (2003)**
- Three-stage theory of multinationality-performance
- Initial costs → learning benefits → over-internationalization costs
- Theoretical foundation for inverted-U shape

**Born-global critique**:
- Some firms internationalize rapidly from inception
- Challenges sequential Uppsala logic
- Your framework acknowledges this through digital lens

### Uppsala in Digital Era (2024-2025)

**Recent empirical tests**:
- Uppsala logic still relevant for learning mechanism
- But speed and sequence altered by digitalization
- Your "re-positioning" approach is appropriate

### Implications for Your Framework

- Uppsala provides process foundation for cost-benefit logic
- Don't reject Uppsala; extend it for digital age
- Learning mechanism remains valid but data-augmented

## 6. Upper Echelons Theory

### Core Works

**Hambrick & Mason (1984)** — Original
- Strategic choices reflect top management characteristics
- Cognitive bases and values matter

**Hambrick (2007)** — Review
- 25 years of upper echelons research
- Expanded to include board and TMT

### Upper Echelons in Internationalization

**Sambharya (1996)**
- TMT international experience affects internationalization
- Relevant for your managerial control variables

**Hsu et al. (2013)**
- CEO characteristics in SME internationalization
- Emerging market context
- Supports your H4 logic

**Post & Byron (2015)** — Gender Diversity
- Meta-analysis of board gender diversity and firm performance
- Positive relationship in complex environments
- Relevant for your gender variable

### Implications for Your Framework

- Upper echelons provides governance layer
- Your treatment as control (not core moderator) is appropriate
- Distinguishes managerial quality from core theoretical mechanisms

## 7. Nonlinearity in I-P Research

### Theoretical Foundations

**Hitt et al. (1997)**
- Inverted-U in product diversification
- Logic extends to international diversification

**Ruigrok & Wagner (2003)**
- Systematic review of I-P shapes
- Linear, U-shape, inverted-U, S-curve all found
- Highlights need for contingency approach

### Recent Developments

**Xiao et al. (2013)**
- Governance moderators in China
- Shape of I-P varies by governance quality
- Relevant for your H5 and H6

**Lind & Mehlum (2010)** — Methodological
- Proper testing of U-shaped relationships
- Your methodology should cite this

### Implications for Your Framework

- H1 inverted-U is theoretically grounded
- But shape likely varies by moderators (H2-H6)
- Need to test both shape and turning point

## 8. Small Island Developing States (SIDS) Literature

### Structural Constraints

**Lall (2004)**
- SIDS face unique challenges: small market, remoteness, vulnerability
- Forced internationalization due to market size
- Theoretical basis for your H1b

**UNCTAD reports (various)**
- Trade costs extremely high for Pacific SIDS
- Limited institutional support
- Structural disadvantages compound

### Implications for Your Framework

- H1b (Forced Internationalization Penalty) is novel
- Addresses boundary condition of H1
- Pacific SIDS as "natural experiment" for institutional extremes

## 9. Asian Business Context

### Regional Diversity

**ASEAN vs. GCC vs. South Asia**:
- Institutional heterogeneity within Asia
- Your ICRV framework captures this

**Emerging vs. Frontier markets**:
- Different stages of development
- Different I-P dynamics

### Implications for Your Framework

- Asian context is underrepresented in meta-analyses
- Your 47-economy coverage fills major gap
- Regional diversity enables institutional gradient analysis

## 10. Methodological References

### Meta-Regression

**Lipsey & Wilson (2001)**
- Practical meta-analysis methods
- Heterogeneity analysis

**Hedges & Olkin (1985)**
- Statistical methods for meta-analysis
- Effect size computation

### Moderation Analysis

**Aiken & West (1991)**
- Multiple regression with interactions
- Plotting and probing interactions

**Hayes (2018)**
- PROCESS macro for moderation/mediation
- Conditional effects analysis

### Panel Data

**Wooldridge (2010)**
- Econometric analysis of cross-section and panel data
- Fixed effects, random effects, dynamic panels

### Implications for Your Framework

- Methodology must align with multi-level, multi-moderator structure
- Need robust methods for curvilinearity + moderation
- Panel structure enables dynamic analysis (H6)
