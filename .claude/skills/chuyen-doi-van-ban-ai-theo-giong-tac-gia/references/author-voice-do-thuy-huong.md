# Author Voice Profile — Do Thuy Huong (Can Tho University)

Calibrated from the author's own work: "Internationalization and Firm Performance:
A Meta-Analysis Review" (Do Thuy Huong & Phan Anh Tu, Can Tho University) and the
2025 UK book chapter on the same topic. Use this profile when applying the
author-voice conversion to manuscripts in this dissertation programme (P1–P8,
thesis chapters, chuyên đề, book chapter).

## Register and stance
- Formal academic English; impersonal **study-as-agent** framing is the default:
  "This study aims to…", "This study employs…", "This study provides…",
  "the findings indicate…". Prefer this over first-person "we"/"I" in
  solo-framed sections. (Co-authored empirical papers may use "we" where the
  surrounding manuscript already does — keep within-document consistency.)
- Moderate, controlled hedging: "depends on", "may", "tends to", "appears to",
  "this impact depends on moderating factors". Avoid both over-hedging and
  flat over-assertion.
- Gap-driven motivation: states what is "lacking", what is "among the few",
  what prior work "has attempted" but not resolved.

## Sentence and paragraph rhythm
- Medium-length, topic-first declarative sentences (~20–30 words), interleaved
  with shorter summarising sentences. Avoid uniform sentence length.
- Connectives used sparingly and naturally: "However,", "Therefore,",
  "While there have been…". Do **not** stack "Firstly / Secondly / Finally".
- Inline enumerated objectives rather than bullet lists:
  "This study aims to (1) … and (2) …".

## Citation habits
- Mixes integral and parenthetical citation:
  integral — "Hunter and Schmidt (1990) suggest that…";
  parenthetical clusters — "(Ruigrok & Wagner, 2004; Bausch & Krist, 2007)".
- Frames the literature as a developmental trajectory (e.g., the three-stage
  research-maturation framing of Hunter & Schmidt).

## Characteristic vocabulary / collocations
- "pivotal", "systematic relationship", "moderating factors/variables",
  "heterogeneity", "uncover the true relationship", "conflicting/contradictory
  results", "comprehensive quantitative analysis", "represents a logical step".
- Domain terms: internationalization, firm performance, FDI, effect size,
  meta-analysis, moderation, country of origin, industry context.

## AI markers to remove (per this author's baseline)
- Mechanical "Firstly/Secondly/Finally" scaffolding.
- Uniform sentence cadence and over-used transition words.
- Em-dash overuse; generic, voice-less "It is important to note that…".
- Over-formal/robotic tone with no critical evaluation or gap framing.

## Application notes
- Process long manuscripts section by section; prioritise Introduction and
  Discussion (where voice is strongest); Methods may stay more impersonal per
  discipline convention.
- Preserve all empirical content, numbers, citations, and APA7 formatting —
  this conversion changes voice, never substance.
