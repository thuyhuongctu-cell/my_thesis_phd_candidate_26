## Sentence structure

- **Length:** medium; typically 20–35 words. Topic sentence followed by one or two
  elaborating clauses. Low variance — steady, measured rhythm rather than dramatic
  short/long alternation.
- **Type:** predominantly complex sentences with one subordinate clause; inline
  enumerations rendered as "(1) … (2) … (3) …" within a single sentence.
- **Punctuation:** commas and semicolons in lists; colons to introduce enumerations.
  Does **not** use em-dashes or arrows (consistent with the Scopus/WoS cleanup).

## Lexicon and connectors

- **Sentence openers (high frequency):** "However," / "Therefore," / "Similarly," /
  "Accordingly," / "In other words," / "This finding …" / "This result …".
- **Evidence framing:** "the findings indicate", "the results demonstrate",
  "it is evident that", "this study goes further by", "consistent with … but expands upon".
- **Domain terms (keep verbatim):** internationalization–firm performance relationship,
  moderating variables/factors, effect size, heterogeneity (I²), combined effect,
  subgroup analysis, publication bias, PRISMA, inverted-U / S-curve, FSTS, TCI, DAI,
  RBV, Uppsala model, ICRV.

## Rhetorical stance

- **Hedging:** low-to-moderate. Hedges where evidence is genuinely mixed ("may depend
  on", "is likely to") but states confirmed results firmly ("H1 is supported",
  "confirming that", "demonstrates"). Avoid stacked hedges (no "may possibly suggest").
- **Voice:** refers to herself as "the author" / "this study"; occasional "we can conclude".
- **Positioning move (signature):** almost every result is positioned against prior
  work — "consistent with X (year); however, this study goes further by …", "did not
  thoroughly analyse". Reproduce this contrast-and-extend pattern.

## Citations

- Mostly **parenthetical** (Author, year), often in multi-citation clusters.
- **Narrative** form when the cited author is the actor ("Higgins et al. (2003) measure …",
  "Geyskens et al. (2009) recommend …").

## Bilingual Vietnamese conventions

- **Register:** Hán-Việt heavy, formal academic Vietnamese.
- **Technical terms:** English term kept inline, often with a Vietnamese gloss on first
  use (e.g., "Quan điểm dựa trên nguồn lực (Resource-Based View, RBV)"). Acronyms in English.
- **Decimals:** comma as decimal separator in BOTH languages (0,07; r = 0,179; p < 0,001) —
  this is a strong, consistent fingerprint. Thousands use a period (101.185).
- **Sentence shape in VI:** influenced by English academic structure (longer, multi-clause)
  rather than short traditional Vietnamese sentences, but stays grammatical and clear.

## Top 5 distinctive features (apply these first)

1. Contrast-and-extend positioning against prior literature in nearly every claim.
2. Firm result statements with low hedging; hedge only genuine ambiguity.
3. Inline "(1)…(2)…(3)…" enumerations instead of bullet fragmentation.
4. Connective openers: However / Therefore / Accordingly / This finding.
5. Decimal comma; English technical terms with Vietnamese gloss on first mention.
