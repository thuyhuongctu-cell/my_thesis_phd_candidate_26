# Discipline Conventions Trong Academic Writing

Các conventions về authorial voice khác nhau đáng kể giữa các disciplines. Tài liệu này giúp adjust voice phù hợp với field của tác giả.

## Natural Sciences

### Voice Characteristics

**Objectivity Emphasis:**
- Minimal self-mention
- Passive voice common ("Experiments were conducted...")
- Focus on methods và results rather than interpretation
- Impersonal constructions preferred

**Vietnamese Example:**
"Các mẫu được thu thập từ ba địa điểm khác nhau và phân tích bằng phương pháp X. Kết quả cho thấy..."

**English Example:**
"Samples were collected from three sites and analyzed using method X. Results indicate..."

### When Voice Appears

- **Methods:** Almost no authorial voice, pure description
- **Results:** Minimal, mostly in interpretation
- **Discussion:** Moderate voice when interpreting significance
- **Introduction:** Some voice when establishing research gap

### Typical Patterns

- Heavy use of nominalizations
- Short, declarative sentences
- Minimal hedging in established facts
- More hedging in interpretations

## Social Sciences

### Voice Characteristics

**Balanced Approach:**
- Moderate self-mention, often "we"
- Mix of active và passive voice
- Clear positioning within theoretical frameworks
- Explicit methodology justification

**Vietnamese Example:**
"Chúng tôi áp dụng phương pháp phân tích nội dung để khám phá các patterns trong dữ liệu. Cách tiếp cận này cho phép chúng tôi..."

**English Example:**
"We applied content analysis to explore patterns in the data. This approach allows us to..."

### Discipline Variations

**Psychology:**
- More objective, closer to natural sciences
- Strong emphasis on methodology
- Cautious claims với extensive hedging

**Sociology:**
- More interpretive voice
- Explicit theoretical positioning
- Critical engagement với literature

**Education:**
- Strong authorial presence
- Reflective elements common
- Practice implications emphasized

**Economics:**
- Technical, model-focused
- Less personal voice
- Strong emphasis on quantitative evidence

## Humanities

### Voice Characteristics

**Strong Authorial Presence:**
- Frequent use of "I" (especially in solo work)
- Active voice dominant
- Interpretive và argumentative stance
- Personal engagement với texts/artifacts

**Vietnamese Example:**
"Tôi cho rằng cách đọc này mở ra một góc nhìn mới về văn bản. Thông qua phân tích chi tiết, tôi sẽ chứng minh..."

**English Example:**
"I argue that this reading opens new perspectives on the text. Through close analysis, I will demonstrate..."

### Discipline Variations

**Literature:**
- Highly interpretive
- Strong personal voice
- Complex sentence structures
- Rich vocabulary

**History:**
- Narrative elements
- Moderate personal voice
- Emphasis on evidence và sources
- Causal argumentation

**Philosophy:**
- Argumentative focus
- Clear logical structure
- Explicit reasoning
- Engagement với opposing views

**Linguistics:**
- More technical than other humanities
- Data-driven (corpus, experiments)
- Moderate personal voice
- Clear examples

## Applied Fields

### Engineering

**Characteristics:**
- Problem-solution structure
- Technical precision
- Minimal personal voice
- Focus on practical applications

**Voice appears in:**
- Design decisions justification
- Limitations discussion
- Future work suggestions

### Medicine và Health Sciences

**Characteristics:**
- Highly structured (IMRAD)
- Cautious claims (patient safety)
- Collective "we" common
- Evidence hierarchy emphasized

**Special Considerations:**
- Ethical implications discussed
- Clinical relevance highlighted
- Limitations explicitly stated

### Business và Management

**Characteristics:**
- Practical orientation
- Managerial implications emphasized
- Moderate personal voice
- Case-based reasoning common

**Voice appears in:**
- Theoretical contribution claims
- Practical recommendations
- Limitations và future research

## Vietnamese Academic Context

### General Tendencies

**Formality:**
- Vietnamese academic writing tends toward high formality
- More formal than equivalent English texts
- Respectful language for citing scholars

**Collectivism:**
- "Chúng tôi" strongly preferred over "tôi"
- Even in solo-authored works
- Reflects cultural values

**Structure:**
- Clear section markers
- Explicit transitions
- Systematic organization valued

### Common Patterns

**Opening Statements:**
"Trong bối cảnh [context], vấn đề [issue] đang ngày càng được quan tâm. Nghiên cứu này nhằm..."

**Literature Review:**
"Nhiều nghiên cứu đã chỉ ra... Tuy nhiên, vẫn còn khoảng trống..."

**Claiming Contribution:**
"Nghiên cứu này đóng góp vào lĩnh vực bằng cách..."

**Hedging:**
"Kết quả có thể cho thấy... Điều này gợi ý rằng..."

## Cross-Disciplinary Considerations

### When Author Publishes Across Fields

Nếu tác giả có papers trong multiple disciplines:

1. **Identify target journal conventions**
   - Check journal's published articles
   - Note voice patterns in that venue

2. **Adjust voice accordingly**
   - More objective for science journals
   - More interpretive for humanities venues

3. **Maintain core identity**
   - Keep signature elements
   - Adapt degree rather than completely change

### Interdisciplinary Writing

For interdisciplinary work:
- Balance conventions from both fields
- Explicitly explain methodological choices
- More metadiscourse to guide diverse readers
- Moderate voice—not too extreme either way

## Regional Variations

### Vietnamese Academic Writing

**Influenced by:**
- French academic tradition (historical)
- Soviet academic model (mid-20th century)
- Anglo-American conventions (recent decades)

**Current trends:**
- Moving toward international standards
- Increasing English publication
- Maintaining Vietnamese characteristics in Vietnamese-language publications

### When Writing for International Journals

Vietnamese authors writing in English:

**Common adjustments needed:**
- Less formal than Vietnamese convention
- More direct claims acceptable
- Less elaborate transitions
- Stronger authorial positioning expected

**Maintain:**
- Rigorous evidence
- Clear structure
- Respectful engagement với literature

## Practical Application

Khi chuyển đổi văn bản, check:

1. **Discipline match:**
   - Voice level appropriate for field?
   - Citation style fits conventions?
   - Structure follows norms?

2. **Cultural context:**
   - Vietnamese vs international audience?
   - Language-specific conventions respected?
   - Formality level appropriate?

3. **Author's typical positioning:**
   - Does transformed voice match author's other work?
   - Is it consistent with their research identity?
   - Does it fit the target venue?

Always prioritize author's established voice over generic discipline conventions when conflict arises—authorial identity matters more than rigid rule-following.
