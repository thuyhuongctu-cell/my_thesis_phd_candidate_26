# Common Theories in International Business Research

Bài nghiên cứu IB thường dựa trên các lý thuyết nền tảng. Tài liệu này tóm tắt các lý thuyết phổ biến nhất và cách áp dụng chúng.

## 1. Internalization Theory (Buckley & Casson, 1976; Rugman, 1981)

**Core idea:** Firms become multinational to internalize market imperfections, especially in knowledge and technology transfer.

**Key concepts:**
- Transaction costs
- Market failure
- Firm-specific advantages (FSAs)

**Khi nào sử dụng:**
- Nghiên cứu về foreign market entry mode choice
- Quyết định outsourcing vs. internalization
- Vertical integration trong value chains quốc tế

**Typical hypotheses:**
- Firms with high R&D intensity are more likely to choose wholly-owned subsidiaries over joint ventures.
- Asset specificity positively influences the degree of internalization.

**Key citations:**
```
Buckley, P. J., & Casson, M. (1976). The future of the multinational enterprise. London: Macmillan.

Rugman, A. M. (1981). Inside the multinationals: The economics of internal markets. New York: Columbia University Press.
```

## 2. Uppsala Internationalization Model (Johanson & Vahlne, 1977, 2009)

**Core idea:** Firms internationalize gradually, increasing commitment as they gain experiential knowledge.

**Key concepts:**
- Psychic distance
- Experiential knowledge
- Incremental commitment
- Establishment chain (no regular export → export via agent → sales subsidiary → production)

**Khi nào sử dụng:**
- Nghiên cứu về internationalization process
- Entry timing và sequence of market entry
- Learning và knowledge accumulation

**Phiên bản 2009 revision:**
- Nhấn mạnh networks và relationships
- Liability of outsidership (thay vì liability of foreignness)

**Typical hypotheses:**
- Firms with more international experience are more likely to enter psychically distant markets.
- Prior experience in similar markets accelerates subsequent market entry.

**Key citations:**
```
Johanson, J., & Vahlne, J.-E. (1977). The internationalization process of the firm: A model of knowledge development and increasing foreign market commitments. Journal of International Business Studies, 8(1), 23-32.

Johanson, J., & Vahlne, J.-E. (2009). The Uppsala internationalization process model revisited: From liability of foreignness to liability of outsidership. Journal of International Business Studies, 40(9), 1411-1431.
```

## 3. Institutional Theory (North, 1990; Scott, 1995)

**Core idea:** Organizations conform to institutional pressures (regulative, normative, cognitive) to gain legitimacy.

**Key concepts:**
- Institutional distance
- Isomorphism (coercive, mimetic, normative)
- Legitimacy
- Formal vs. informal institutions

**Khi nào sử dụng:**
- Nghiên cứu về subsidiary adaptation vs. standardization
- Impact of host country institutions
- Liability of foreignness
- Corporate governance và CSR across countries

**Three pillars of institutions (Scott, 1995):**
- Regulative: Laws, regulations
- Normative: Values, norms
- Cognitive: Taken-for-granted beliefs

**Typical hypotheses:**
- Greater institutional distance increases the likelihood of local adaptation.
- Firms facing strong coercive pressures are more likely to adopt local practices.

**Key citations:**
```
North, D. C. (1990). Institutions, institutional change and economic performance. Cambridge: Cambridge University Press.

Scott, W. R. (1995). Institutions and organizations. Thousand Oaks, CA: Sage.

Kostova, T., & Zaheer, S. (1999). Organizational legitimacy under conditions of complexity: The case of the multinational enterprise. Academy of Management Review, 24(1), 64-81.
```

## 4. Resource-Based View (RBV) (Barney, 1991; Peteraf, 1993)

**Core idea:** Sustainable competitive advantage comes from valuable, rare, inimitable, and non-substitutable (VRIN) resources.

**Key concepts:**
- VRIN resources
- Dynamic capabilities
- Knowledge-based view (extension)

**Khi nào sử dụng:**
- Nghiên cứu về competitive advantage của MNCs
- Knowledge transfer và innovation
- Subsidiary capabilities

**Typical hypotheses:**
- Firms with superior technological capabilities achieve better performance in foreign markets.
- Knowledge-based resources positively moderate the relationship between internationalization and performance.

**Key citations:**
```
Barney, J. (1991). Firm resources and sustained competitive advantage. Journal of Management, 17(1), 99-120.

Peteraf, M. A. (1993). The cornerstones of competitive advantage: A resource-based view. Strategic Management Journal, 14(3), 179-191.
```

## 5. Eclectic Paradigm (OLI Framework) (Dunning, 1980, 1988)

**Core idea:** FDI occurs when firms possess Ownership, Location, and Internalization advantages.

**Key concepts:**
- **O**wnership advantages: Firm-specific assets
- **L**ocation advantages: Host country attractiveness
- **I**nternalization advantages: Benefits of internal control vs. market transactions

**Khi nào sử dụng:**
- Nghiên cứu về FDI determinants
- Location choice
- Comprehensive framework combining multiple factors

**Typical hypotheses:**
- Firms with strong ownership advantages are more likely to engage in FDI.
- Location advantages of the host country positively influence FDI inflows.

**Key citations:**
```
Dunning, J. H. (1980). Toward an eclectic theory of international production: Some empirical tests. Journal of International Business Studies, 11(1), 9-31.

Dunning, J. H. (1988). The eclectic paradigm of international production: A restatement and some possible extensions. Journal of International Business Studies, 19(1), 1-31.
```

## 6. Transaction Cost Economics (TCE) (Williamson, 1975, 1985)

**Core idea:** Firms choose governance structures that minimize transaction costs.

**Key concepts:**
- Asset specificity
- Uncertainty
- Frequency
- Bounded rationality
- Opportunism

**Khi nào sử dụng:**
- Entry mode choice (equity vs. non-equity)
- Make-or-buy decisions
- Governance of international alliances

**Typical hypotheses:**
- Higher asset specificity increases the likelihood of equity-based entry modes.
- Environmental uncertainty positively influences the preference for flexible governance structures.

**Key citations:**
```
Williamson, O. E. (1975). Markets and hierarchies: Analysis and antitrust implications. New York: Free Press.

Williamson, O. E. (1985). The economic institutions of capitalism. New York: Free Press.
```

## 7. Springboard Perspective (Luo & Tung, 2007)

**Core idea:** EMNEs use international expansion as a springboard to acquire strategic assets and overcome latecomer disadvantages.

**Key concepts:**
- Asset-seeking FDI
- Accelerated internationalization
- Leapfrogging

**Khi nào sử dụng:**
- Nghiên cứu về emerging market multinationals
- Outward FDI from developing countries
- Acquisition strategies

**Typical hypotheses:**
- EMNEs are more likely to acquire firms in developed markets to access advanced technology.
- State support positively influences EMNEs' springboard behavior.

**Key citations:**
```
Luo, Y., & Tung, R. L. (2007). International expansion of emerging market enterprises: A springboard perspective. Journal of International Business Studies, 38(4), 481-498.
```

## 8. Liability of Foreignness (Zaheer, 1995; Hymer, 1976)

**Core idea:** Foreign firms face additional costs compared to local firms due to unfamiliarity, discrimination, and distance.

**Key concepts:**
- Spatial distance costs
- Unfamiliarity costs
- Discrimination by host country stakeholders
- Home country costs

**Khi nào sử dụng:**
- Performance of foreign subsidiaries
- Strategies to overcome foreignness
- Subsidiary survival

**Typical hypotheses:**
- Liability of foreignness negatively affects subsidiary performance.
- Local partnerships mitigate the liability of foreignness.

**Key citations:**
```
Zaheer, S. (1995). Overcoming the liability of foreignness. Academy of Management Journal, 38(2), 341-363.

Hymer, S. H. (1976). The international operations of national firms: A study of direct foreign investment. Cambridge, MA: MIT Press.
```

## Cách Kết Hợp Nhiều Lý Thuyết

Nghiên cứu IB hiện đại thường kết hợp nhiều theoretical lenses để có góc nhìn toàn diện hơn.

**Ví dụ 1: TCE + Institutional Theory**
- TCE giải thích efficiency considerations trong entry mode choice
- Institutional theory giải thích legitimacy considerations
- Combined: Entry mode choice là kết quả của cả efficiency và legitimacy pressures

**Ví dụ 2: RBV + Uppsala Model**
- RBV xác định resources cần thiết cho internationalization
- Uppsala model giải thích process of building experiential knowledge
- Combined: Firms leverage existing resources while building new knowledge through gradual expansion

**Ví dụ 3: OLI + Springboard**
- OLI framework cho developed country MNCs
- Springboard perspective cho EMNEs
- Combined: Different logics of internationalization for firms from different contexts

## Lưu Ý Khi Sử dụng Theory

1. **Không chỉ cite theory, mà phải apply nó:** Giải thích rõ ràng làm thế nào theory giúp bạn develop hypotheses

2. **Acknowledge limitations của theory:** Không có theory nào hoàn hảo cho mọi context

3. **Update với recent developments:** Nhiều theories có phiên bản revised (ví dụ Uppsala 2009, OLI paradigm extensions)

4. **Context matters:** Một số theories phù hợp hơn với certain contexts (ví dụ Springboard cho EMNEs, Uppsala cho SMEs)

5. **Theoretical contribution:** Bài nghiên cứu tốt không chỉ test theory mà còn extend hoặc refine nó
