## METHODS
| # | Item | Checklist |
|---|------|-----------|
| 5 | Eligibility criteria | Specify inclusion and exclusion criteria for the review and how studies were grouped for the syntheses. |
| 6 | Information sources | Specify all databases, registers, websites, organisations, reference lists and other sources searched or consulted to identify studies. Specify the date when each source was last searched or consulted. |
| 7 | Search strategy | Present the full search strategies for all databases, registers and websites, including any filters and limits used. |
| 8 | Selection process | Specify methods used to decide whether a study met the inclusion criteria, including how many reviewers screened each record and each report retrieved, whether they worked independently, and if applicable, details of automation tools used. |
| 9 | Data collection process | Specify methods used to collect data from reports, including how many reviewers collected data, whether they worked independently, any processes for obtaining/confirming data from study investigators, and any automation tools used. |
| 10a | Data items (outcomes) | List and define all outcomes for which data were sought. |
| 10b | Data items (other variables) | List and define all other variables for which data were sought. |
| 11 | Study risk of bias assessment | Specify the methods used to assess RoB in the included studies. |
| 12 | Effect measures | Specify for each outcome the effect measure(s) used. |
| 13a | Synthesis methods (eligibility) | Describe the processes used to decide which studies were eligible for each synthesis. |
| 13b | Synthesis methods (data preparation) | Describe methods required to prepare the data for presentation/synthesis. |
| 13c | Synthesis methods (tabulation) | Describe methods used to tabulate/visually display results. |
| 13d | Synthesis methods (synthesis) | Describe methods used to synthesise results and rationale. If meta-analysis, describe model(s), heterogeneity methods, software. |
| 13e | Synthesis methods (heterogeneity) | Describe methods to explore possible causes of heterogeneity. |
| 13f | Synthesis methods (sensitivity) | Describe sensitivity analyses. |
| 14 | Reporting bias assessment | Describe methods used to assess risk of bias due to missing results. |
| 15 | Certainty assessment | Describe methods to assess certainty in the body of evidence (e.g. GRADE). |

## RESULTS
| # | Item | Checklist |
|---|------|-----------|
| 16a | Study selection | Describe results of search and selection, from records identified to studies included, ideally using a flow diagram. |
| 16b | Study selection (exclusions) | Cite studies that might appear to meet inclusion but were excluded, and explain why. |
| 17 | Study characteristics | Cite each included study and present its characteristics. |
| 18 | Risk of bias in studies | Present RoB assessments for each included study. |
| 19 | Results of individual studies | Present summary statistics and effect estimates. |
| 20a | Results of syntheses (characteristics) | Briefly summarise characteristics and RoB among contributing studies. |
| 20b | Results of syntheses (statistical) | Present results of statistical syntheses (summary estimates, heterogeneity, direction of effect). |
| 20c | Results of syntheses (heterogeneity) | Present results of investigations of heterogeneity. |
| 20d | Results of syntheses (sensitivity) | Present results of sensitivity analyses. |
| 21 | Reporting biases | Present assessments of reporting bias risks for each synthesis. |
| 22 | Certainty of evidence | Present certainty (e.g. GRADE) for each outcome. |

| # | Item | Checklist |

| 23d | Discussion (implications) | Implications for practice, policy, future research. |
