Dear Editors of Asian Business & Management,

Please consider our manuscript "At the Upper Bound of the Institutional Gradient:
Internationalization and Firm Performance in Japan's Inaugural World Bank
Enterprise Survey" for publication as an original research article.

The paper exploits a unique data event: Japan's first-ever inclusion in the World
Bank Enterprise Surveys (2025; N = 2,168 establishments). This closes the last
major gap in harmonised firm-level data for advanced Asia and allows the first
direct test, on identical instrumentation, of how the internationalization-
performance relationship behaves at the upper bound of the institutional
gradient. We find a monotonically positive relationship with no detectable
turning point, level-shifting (not curve-reshaping) effects of technological
capability and digital adoption, a firm-longevity premium consistent with the
shinise tradition, and a strikingly thin female-leadership margin.

The manuscript is approximately 4,800 words, has not been published, and is not
under consideration elsewhere. A full replication script accompanies the
submission. Given the journal's focus on the Asian business context and on
Japan in particular, we believe the paper will interest your readership.
