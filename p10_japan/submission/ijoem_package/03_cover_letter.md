Dear Editors of International Journal of Emerging Markets,

Please consider our manuscript "At the Upper Bound of the Institutional Gradient:
Internationalization, Capabilities, and Firm Performance in Japan's Inaugural
World Bank Enterprise Survey" for publication as an original research article.

The paper exploits a unique data event: Japan's first-ever inclusion in the World
Bank Enterprise Surveys (2025; N = 2,168 establishments). This closes the last
major gap in harmonised firm-level data for advanced Asia and allows the first
direct test, on identical instrumentation, of how the internationalization-
performance relationship behaves at the upper bound of the institutional
gradient. The setting is theoretically pointed: the inverted-U that organises
this literature was originally established on Japanese firms (Lu & Beamish,
2001, 2004), yet Japan has never been observed on the harmonised instrument that
benchmarks it against its regional peers. We find a monotonically positive
relationship with no detectable turning point across eleven specifications;
level-shifting (not curve-reshaping) effects of technological capability and
digital adoption; and, most consequentially, that the productivity payoff to
internationalization operates at the extensive margin rather than along the
intensity gradient: foreign ownership, technological capability, and digital
adoption govern who exports, and exporters earn a 25.8% productivity premium. We
also document a firm-longevity premium consistent with the shinise tradition and
a strikingly thin female-leadership margin.

The manuscript is approximately 7,000 words, has not been published, and is not
under consideration elsewhere. A full replication script accompanies the
submission. Given the journal's focus on the Asian business context and on
Japan in particular, we believe the paper will interest your readership.
