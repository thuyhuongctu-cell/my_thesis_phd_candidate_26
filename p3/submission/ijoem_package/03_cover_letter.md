**Submission of Manuscript: "Revisiting the Internationalisation–Performance Relationship in an Emerging Market: The Roles of Technological Capability and Foundational Digital Adoption"**

Dear Editor-in-Chief,

We are pleased to submit the above manuscript for consideration for publication in the *International Journal of Emerging Markets* (IJOEM). The manuscript presents firm-level evidence on the internationalisation–performance (I–P) relationship using three waves of World Bank Enterprise Survey data for Vietnam (2009, 2015, 2023; N = 2,958).

**Relevance to IJOEM**

The study sits squarely within IJOEM's core interest in firm-level dynamics in emerging and transition economies. Vietnam is a lower-middle-income transition economy whose institutional trajectory spans WTO accession (2007), CPTPP ratification (2019), and the post-COVID digital-economy push (2023); the multi-wave design lets us test whether the curvilinear I–P relationship is structurally durable across more than a decade of institutional change, a question central to IJOEM's readership. The study also clarifies how foundational digital adoption operates differently from technological capability in an emerging-market setting.

**Key Contributions**

First, the inverted-U relationship between export intensity and labour productivity is structurally durable: turning points cluster at 39–46% of direct-export intensity across all three waves (Lind–Mehlum p ≤ .013 per wave; p < .001 pooled). Second, the study separates technological capability (TCI) from foundational digital adoption (DAI, Tier-1 website proxy) as non-overlapping constructs; TCI is robustly positive across waves, whereas the website-based digital indicator follows a trajectory consistent with Tier-1 proxy obsolescence. Third, Paternoster cross-wave z-tests confirm that the shifts in the digital-adoption association are statistically distinguishable, with direct implications for digital-economy programmes in transition economies.
