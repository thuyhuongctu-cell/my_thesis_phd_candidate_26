## Hệ quả học thuật

**Đây không phải vấn đề "vi phạm liêm chính"** — việc tác giả đề xuất một khung lý thuyết mới là chuẩn mực trong nghiên cứu IB (mọi đóng góp lý thuyết bắt đầu bằng "we propose"). Nhưng có 3 hàm ý quan trọng:

### Hàm ý 1: Cách phrasing trong manuscripts cần cẩn thận

| Phrasing có rủi ro | Phrasing an toàn |
|---|---|
| ❌ "Drawing on CIMT (Kogut & Zander, 1993; Khanna & Palepu, 2010)" — implies CIMT là lý thuyết của họ | ✅ "We propose the Capability-Institution Mismatch mechanism, integrating insights from Kogut & Zander (1993), Zaheer (1995), Peng et al. (2008), and Khanna & Palepu (2010)" |
| ❌ "CIMT predicts that..." (without qualifier) | ✅ "We extend prior institutional and resource-based work to propose that..." |
| ❌ Calling it "**Theory**" (technical term in IB requires formal axiomatic structure) | ✅ Calling it "**mechanism**", "**framework**", or "**logic**" |

### Hàm ý 2: Defense committee questions to anticipate

- *"Em coining 'CIMT' nhằm mục đích gì? Đây có phải lý thuyết hoàn chỉnh hay là một synthesis mechanism?"*

Top journal reviewers sẽ đặt:
- *"The authors' CIMT is essentially a relabeling of institutional voids theory (Khanna & Palepu, 2010) combined with LoF (Zaheer, 1995). What is the novel theoretical contribution beyond integration?"*
- *"Without independent published-paper support, 'we propose CIMT' is a strong claim. Why not frame as 'integrated mechanism' or 'analytical framework' to match the actual contribution?"*

## Đề xuất chỉnh sửa cụ thể

- P6: "Capability–Institution Mismatch Theory (CIMT)" → "**Capability–Institution Mismatch (CIM) mechanism**" hoặc "**CIM framework**"
- P7: Tương tự

Formal empirical confirmation of all three sub-mechanisms in a single design is a goal of the present paper; the theoretical proposition stands on the established foundations of the constituent literatures."*

# CÂU HỎI 4: REVIEW VAI TRÒ TOP-1% IB SCHOLAR (with APA 7th citations)

## Reviewer Profile

I review this dissertation portfolio as a senior International Business scholar with publications in *Journal of International Business Studies*, *Strategic Management Journal*, and *Academy of Management Journal*, specializing in institutional theory and emerging-market firm strategy. My review applies the standards of top-1% IB scholarship.

## Overall Assessment

The dissertation portfolio (P1-P9' + book chapter + ICBEF meta + 2 chuyên đề + 5 chapters) demonstrates a **methodologically ambitious and theoretically integrative** research program centered on the internationalization-performance (I-P) relationship in Asia and the Pacific. The empirical scale (45 economies, ~165,000 firm-observations, ~14 years of WBES data) is impressive and rare in IB scholarship. The pre-registration of the meta-analysis (OSF z37kn) reflects best-practice methodological transparency that exceeds the IB field standard (Aguinis et al., 2018).

However, three substantive concerns warrant attention before submission to top journals.

### Concern 1: Theoretical contribution — CIMT framing risks overstatement

The authors propose "Capability-Institution Mismatch Theory" (CIMT) as a unifying mechanism integrating three established sub-mechanisms: rent protection (Kogut & Zander, 1993; Zaheer, 1995), liability-of-foreignness attenuation (Peng, Wang, & Jiang, 2008; Zaheer, 1995), and institutional-void amplification (Khanna & Palepu, 1997, 2010). Each sub-mechanism is well-grounded. However, the use of "Theory" rather than "Framework" or "Mechanism" requires careful defense.

In IB scholarship, a theory is typically defined by Whetten's (1989) four elements — *what* (constructs), *how* (relationships), *why* (rationale), and *who-where-when* (contextual boundaries) — and validated through cumulative empirical replication across multiple independent research programs (Bacharach, 1989; Suddaby, 2010). The proposed CIMT meets *what* and *how* requirements but is, at present, a single-author-program theoretical synthesis without independent replication. The conservative scholarly framing would be:

> *"We synthesise prior work on rent protection (Kogut & Zander, 1993), liability of foreignness (Zaheer, 1995), and institutional voids (Khanna & Palepu, 2010) into an integrated capability-institution mismatch mechanism..."*

This avoids the rhetorical risk of "theory inflation" (Suddaby, 2014) while preserving the substantive contribution.

### Concern 2: Cross-paper theoretical inconsistency — book chapter vs P9' India

The portfolio includes a published book chapter (Đỗ & Phan, 2025, IntechOpen, https://doi.org/10.5772/intechopen.1011012) analysing Indian firm performance through the Uppsala model (Johanson & Vahlne, 1977, 2009) and Upper Echelons Theory (Hambrick & Mason, 1984) lens, with a matched sub-sample of 380 firms. The present P9' India submission adopts a Conditional Digital Capability Moderation (CDCM) framework on the full WBES India sample (N = 28,717). This theoretical shift is substantive and requires explicit justification beyond "different scope". A top journal reviewer would ask:

> *"What changed in the authors' theoretical reasoning between 2025 and 2026 that motivated the framework shift? Is CDCM a natural extension of Upper Echelons logic in the Indian context, or a substitution?"*

This is addressable through a clear theoretical-progression narrative in P9' §1.

### Concern 3: ICBEF 2025 meta-analysis as "baseline" creates blind-review tension

P6 §1 cites a prior author's meta-analysis ("the ICBEF 2025 Asia-Pacific baseline, k = 113") as the empirical foundation for the present k = 238 expansion. In blind review, the journal cannot disclose this self-citation explicitly. However, a sophisticated reviewer will likely identify the connection through citation pattern analysis. The standard approach is to rephrase as "a prior Asia-Pacific meta-analytic baseline" without naming the conference (ICBEF) or year (2025). I recommend this rephrasing for P6.

## Specific Theoretical Concerns

### On the integration of Uppsala, RBV, Institutional Theory, and Upper Echelons

The dissertation's "4-tier integrated framework" follows the IB literature tradition of multi-theoretic integration (Peng et al., 2008; Cuervo-Cazurra et al., 2018). This approach is well-established and reviewer-friendly. However, the addition of CIMT as a 5th unifying mechanism *plus* ICRV taxonomy *plus* CDCM construct distinction creates **theoretical inflation** that may concern Whetten's (1989) parsimony criterion.

The pre-CIMT framework (5 labels) is more defensible than the post-CIMT framework (7 labels). The trade-off is between:
- **Parsimony** (pre-CIMT framework): Fewer labels, cleaner argument
- **Theoretical contribution** (post-CIMT framework): Original named mechanism, but risk of "theory inflation" critique

Either choice is defensible, but **internal consistency is critical**. The dissertation Ch.2 and CĐ2 currently use pre-CIMT framework while P6 and P7 use post-CIMT framework. This inconsistency must be resolved before defense.

### On the Uppsala model "re-positioning" claim

The dissertation claims to "re-position Uppsala for the AI era, from experiential learning to data-augmented learning." This is a strong claim. Uppsala has been "re-positioned" multiple times since 1977: Johansson & Vahlne (2009) themselves added "outsider" framing; Vahlne and Johanson (2020) added micro-foundations; Coviello et al. (2017) added born-global revisions. The dissertation's re-positioning needs to specify:
- How does data-augmented learning differ from experiential learning operationally?
- What empirical signature distinguishes the two?
- Is this re-positioning theoretical or simply contextualizing Uppsala to a new empirical domain?

Without these specifics, the re-positioning claim risks reading as "label updating" rather than theoretical contribution.

### On the I-P meta-analysis literature anchor

The dissertation cites five anchor meta-analyses:
- Bausch and Krist (2007)
- Kirca et al. (2012)
- Marano et al. (2016)
- Wu et al. (2022)
- Arte and Larimo (2022)

In P6 §1, Schwens et al. (2018) is cited as a sixth "anchor" but is in fact an outsourcing meta-analysis, not an I-P meta-analysis. This is a real methodological issue (already flagged in p6/RECONCILIATION_NOTES.md item #8). The cleanest fix is to either:
(a) Remove Schwens et al. (2018) from the "I-P meta-analyses" framing
(b) Add a clarifying note that Schwens et al. (2018) was used as a methodological anchor for backward citation tracking, not as an I-P meta-analysis substantive precedent

## Methodological Concerns

### On the meta-analysis single-coder limitation

The P6 meta-analysis acknowledges single-coder extraction with mitigations (pilot calibration, double-entry verification, mechanical reproducibility of ICRV/cDAI/DPL codes). This is transparent and follows PRISMA 2020 (Page et al., 2021) Item 9 requirements for disclosure. However, the absence of a κ statistic limits the credibility of moderator coding decisions for variables requiring judgment (e.g., ICRV coding for borderline economies).

The cover letter offers Path B as a planned reliability check. This is a reasonable mitigation but insufficient for top-journal acceptance at MIR or APJM. I recommend the authors recruit a second coder for at least a 10% random subsample of the current k = 238 corpus before submission. This is a finite-effort intervention (5-10 hours for the second coder) that materially strengthens the manuscript's methodological credibility.

### On the inverted-U identification in country-specific papers

P3 Vietnam (step-function at participation margin), P4 Singapore (high-tail-only DAI moderation), and P5 China (structural stability of TP ≈ 48%) each demonstrate the inverted-U or its modification. However, the cross-paper turning points (Vietnam 39%, China 47.8%, Singapore 82%) need synthesis. The current dissertation Ch.4 presents these as independent findings; the top-journal reviewer will demand a unified explanation of why TP varies across these economies.

The Capability-Institution Mismatch / ICRV framework provides this explanation (TP migrates as institutional quality varies), but the dissertation Ch.4 has not yet integrated this synthesis. This is a primary opportunity to strengthen the contribution.

### On P8 SIDS — Fiji-driven result

The Pacific SIDS analysis demonstrates a Forced Internationalization Penalty (FIP) — internationalisation is monotone-negative with no inverted-U. The leave-one-country-out sensitivity analysis (p8 §4.5 M_LOO) reveals that dropping Fiji weakens the result substantially (β = -0.171, p = .383), while dropping Timor-Leste (despite being 26.8% of the analysis sample) strengthens the result. This is empirically interesting but raises a substantive question:

> *"Is FIP a SIDS-wide structural phenomenon, or is it the Fijian pattern dressed in regional clothing?"*

The current P8 §5.6 limitations acknowledge this empirically but provide no theoretical explanation for why Fiji is so influential. I recommend a 150-word theoretical paragraph addressing Fiji's distinctive institutional features (e.g., Fiji has more developed banking and legal infrastructure than Kiribati or Solomon Islands, which may attenuate FIP relative to extreme SIDS contexts).

## On the OSF Pre-Registration

The OSF pre-registration (https://osf.io/z37kn, DOI: 10.17605/OSF.IO/Z37KN) is a major methodological strength. The transparency of disclosed deviations (5 documented deviations in P6 §3.1) reflects best-practice. However, the cover letter to APJM should explicitly note that the pre-registration is journal-agnostic and that the current submission is on the Path A corpus (k = 238) as registered, with Path B (k ≈ 600-700) as a pre-registered follow-up replication. This framing protects the authors from "p-hacking" concerns.
