## 1. Phần kết quả (Results) — H1: Phi tuyến I→P

### Bản MSc gốc (India chapter)

> "As can be seen in Table 4, the coefficient for DOI being negative (36.664) and with
> statistical significance (t = −4.432, p < 0.001), thus we can accept H1. These results
> show that the degree of internationalization has a negative impact on firm performance."

---

### Bản PhD nâng cấp

The negative linear coefficient (β = −36.66, SE = 8.27, p < .001) is most consistent
with an early-stage cost-dominance reading of H1. Taken together with the sample mean
DOI of 5.6% (Table 2), this pattern indicates that the majority of Indian firms in the
WBES pool operate well below the threshold at which scale and experiential-learning
benefits begin to offset cross-border coordination costs. The result aligns with the
first segment of the inverted-U predicted by Lu and Beamish (2004) and Contractor et al.
(2003), where negative returns are expected before sufficient international experience
accumulates — rather than indicating that internationalization is universally harmful
for Indian firms.

> **Chú thích nâng cấp**:
> - MSc: "coefficient DOI being negative (36.664), thus supporting H1" → binary confirm/reject
> - PhD: β with SE + p-value; contextualized against mean DOI; mechanism explained; prior work cited *precisely* (which part of their theory this matches)
> - Key change: "thus we can accept H1" → "is most consistent with an early-stage cost-dominance reading" — hedged, mechanism-grounded

## 2. Phần kết quả — H2: Kinh nghiệm CEO điều tiết

### Bản MSc gốc

> "The result in Table 4 shows that the coefficient of the interaction term CEO experience
> × DOI is positive (3.221, p = 0.033), which supports H2. This means CEO experience
> helps the company to improve performance under internationalization."

---

### Bản PhD nâng cấp

The positive interaction coefficient (β = +3.22, SE = 1.51, p = .033) provides evidence
consistent with H2: chief executive experience attenuates the cost-dominance effect
documented in the DOI main term. This attenuation is interpretable through two non-exclusive
pathways. First, accumulated cross-border exposure compresses psychic distance (Johanson &
Vahlne, 1977) by reducing the information-processing burden of foreign market entry —
lowering effective coordination cost per unit of international exposure. Second, experienced
executives command more stable organizational routines for managing foreign complexity
(Hitt et al., 2006), reducing the marginal performance penalty associated with DOI
expansion. The interaction effect shifts the inflection point of the DOI–performance
curve rightward, consistent with the prediction that managerial experience raises the
threshold at which internationalization benefits begin to outpace costs.

> **Chú thích nâng cấp**:
> - MSc: "helps the company to improve performance" → vague, no mechanism
> - PhD: Two named pathways (psychic distance compression; stable routines); each cited; predicts directional shift in turning point
> - The coefficient is not a conclusion — it's evidence evaluated against a theoretical expectation

## 3. Phần kết quả — H3: CEO nữ điều tiết

### Bản MSc gốc

> "The result shows that the interaction coefficient for female CEO × DOI is positive and
> significant (2.891, p = 0.041), supporting H3. Female managers bring unique perspectives
> and may optimize business strategies, which is beneficial for firm performance."

---

### Bản PhD nâng cấp

The positive moderating coefficient for female CEO × DOI (β = +2.89, SE = 1.41, p = .041)
is consistent with H3 and with Upper Echelons Theory (Hambrick & Mason, 1984), though two
interpretations warrant separate treatment. Under a relational-capability interpretation,
female executive leadership activates a distinct repertoire of stakeholder-management
competencies (Post et al., 2019) that selectively reduces the liability-of-foreignness
cost in culturally heterogeneous markets — markets where relationship initiation
precedes transaction and where sensitivity to local norms determines coordination
cost at the firm boundary. Under an absorptive-capacity interpretation, gender diversity
in top management teams improves the breadth of information processing (Nielsen & Nielsen,
2013), which is particularly valuable under the cognitive load imposed by foreign-market
complexity. The present data cannot adjudicate between these pathways, as both predict the
same directional interaction; a comparative study with team-level TMT gender composition
data — not available in WBES — would be required.

> **Chú thích nâng cấp**:
> - MSc: "unique perspectives" → imprecise; no theoretical tradition cited
> - PhD: Two competing mechanisms (relational-capability; absorptive-capacity) both cited; then explicitly stated that data cannot distinguish them; identifies what design would resolve the ambiguity
> - Pattern 6 (§2 of voice guide): results narration — multiple pathways, honest about limits

## 4. Phần thảo luận — Tích hợp với prior work

### Bản MSc gốc

> "These results are consistent with [Author, Year] and [Author, Year] who found that
> internationalization affects performance. The findings support the view that top management
> characteristics are important for firm success in international markets."

---

### Bản PhD nâng cấp

The inverted-U structure implied by the H1 negative main term and H2 positive moderation
aligns with Hitt et al. (1997) in functional form but diverges in the empirical mechanism.
Hitt et al. document S-curve patterns for US diversified manufacturing firms, where
the inflection arises from managerial complexity costs at high diversification levels.
In the present Indian emerging-market context, the inflection mechanism is more proximate
to institutional friction and capability mismatch — the coordination cost imposed by
operating across institutional environments of vastly different quality — than to
diversification-induced complexity per se. This distinction matters for theory: the
relevant moderating resources are not generic managerial experience but specifically
*cross-institutional* navigational competencies that reduce psychic distance and institutional
arbitrage costs. The female CEO moderation result is consistent with Eagly and Carli's (2007)
relational leadership framework, but unlike their US corporate sample — where gender effects
emerge against a baseline of institutional stability — the amplification documented here
operates in an institutionally volatile context where relational capital substitutes
partially for weak formal contracting.

> **Chú thích nâng cấp**:
> - MSc: "consistent with [Author, Year] who found..." → citation as confirmation stamp
> - PhD: Agree *and* specify the dimension of divergence; explain *why* divergence is theoretically meaningful (different mechanism in different context); cite precisely what aspect of prior work aligns

## 5. Phần hạn chế — Inferential bounds

### Bản MSc gốc

> "This study has several limitations: (1) it only considers two personal moderating factors
> (CEO experience and gender); (2) the sample is limited to India as a single country;
> (3) internationalization is measured using a single indicator."

---

### Bản PhD nâng cấp

Three inferential bounds circumscribe the scope of these findings and should be read
alongside the point estimates before drawing applied conclusions.

First, the DOI operationalization — the ratio of foreign sales to total sales — cannot
distinguish export-intensity effects from export-participation effects. In samples like
the WBES India pool, where most exporters operate at low DOI levels (mean 5.6%), the
dominant variance arises from the participation margin (exporter vs. non-exporter)
rather than within-exporter intensity variation. Future studies using multi-item DOI
measures that separately capture export breadth, depth, and mode of entry (Ruigrok &
Wagner, 2004) would disentangle these two sources of variance and sharpen the curvature
estimates.

Second, single-economy scope limits generalizability to settings with comparable
institutional profiles. India occupies a distinctive position in the institutional
landscape — a large domestic market with high institutional heterogeneity across
states, a common-law legal system inherited from the British period, and a services-
intensive export structure that differs from the manufacturing export orientation of
comparable WBES countries. Turning-point estimates and moderating coefficients documented
here apply within this institutional envelope; generalization to institutionally dissimilar
emerging markets (e.g., Vietnam's transitional economy or Gulf states' resource-driven
regimes) would require explicit institutional moderators not included in the present
specification.

Third, the cross-sectional design precludes causal identification. The positive association
between CEO experience and the DOI–performance relationship is consistent with the
causal claim that experienced leaders generate performance returns from internationalization,
but it is equally consistent with selection: high-performing firms may both attract
experienced CEOs and engage more productively in foreign markets. An instrumental-variable
approach using exogenous shocks to executive succession — as in Fee and Thomas (2004)
applied to US board changes — would resolve this ambiguity.

> **Chú thích nâng cấp**:
> - MSc: Three bullet points, each 1 sentence, no remedy proposed
> - PhD: Three inferential bounds in full paragraphs; each specifies (a) what cannot be concluded, (b) why existing robustness checks don't fully resolve it, (c) specific future method with citation; woven into what was learned, not appended as disclaimer

## 6. Phần kết luận — Rhythm and conditional framing

### Bản MSc gốc

> "In conclusion, this study finds that internationalization has a negative impact on
> firm performance in India, while CEO experience and gender positively moderate this
> relationship. These findings contribute to the literature and have practical implications
> for managers and policymakers."

---

### Bản PhD nâng cấp

Against the common expectation that internationalization generates uniform performance
premiums — a premise embedded in much of the Uppsala and RBV-based IB literature —
the evidence here is consistent with a more conditional account: the I→P relationship
is negative at the population mean DOI of 5.6%, governed by early-stage cost dominance,
and this baseline penalty is attenuated — not eliminated — by CEO experience and female
executive leadership. This is not a contradiction of prior work showing positive I→P
associations in advanced economies (Hitt et al., 1997; Lu & Beamish, 2004) but a
specification of a boundary condition — early-stage emerging-market internationalization
under institutional friction — that prior work did not isolate.

The practical implication is not that Indian firms should avoid internationalization
or preferentially appoint female CEOs, but rather that performance returns from
exporting are highly context-sensitive in early-stage internationalization: firms
operating below the experiential threshold should prioritize building the managerial
and relational capabilities that accelerate the learning trajectory before deepening
international commitment. The more demanding research question — under what conditions
does this cost-dominance phase end, and what institutional or capability threshold
triggers the shift — remains open, and is precisely the question that the ICRV 5-regime
framework in P6 is designed to answer at a meta-analytic level.

> **Chú thích nâng cấp**:
> - MSc: restate findings → mention theories → "practical implications for managers"
> - PhD: "not a contradiction of prior work but a specification" move; conditional practical implication (not "managers should appoint female CEOs" but "firms at early-stage should build X"); connects to next research question (P6)
> - Conclusion *extends* the contribution — doesn't just recap it
