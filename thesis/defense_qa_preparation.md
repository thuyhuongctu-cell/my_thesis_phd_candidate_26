## How to use this document

Each question below carries:
- **Question (Vietnamese / English)** — likely phrasing
- **Why this question** — the examiner concern behind it
- **Anchor answer** — 60–90 second response (what to say first)
- **Backup material** — supporting numbers and citations to deploy if probed
- **Trap to avoid** — the wrong response that will deepen the concern

Rehearse the **anchor answers** until they are fluent. The **backup material** is fallback content for follow-up probing.

### Q1.3: "Phát hiện thiên lệch công bố ~53% có làm vô hiệu chính chương trình kiểm định moderator của bạn không?" / "Doesn't the ~53% publication bias finding undermine your own moderator project?"

**Why this question:** This is the sharpest internal tension in P6 (the "paradox" paragraph I added in §5.2). An examiner with publication-bias expertise will press here.

**Anchor answer:** *"Đó chính là nghịch lý mà chúng tôi register chứ không spin. Hàm ý là (a) phát hiện moderator giữa các nghiên cứu cần công lực được hiệu chuẩn theo r ≈ .035 thay vì r ≈ .07–.10, làm tăng đáng kể ngưỡng k cho các kiểm định có công lực đủ; và (b) các phân tích tổng hợp I-P tương lai nên báo cáo ước lượng đã hiệu chỉnh thiên lệch như suy luận chính. Bài viết ghi nhận nghịch lý này như một cam kết chương trình nghiên cứu — không phải giải quyết trong một bài đơn lẻ."* *(English: "That is the paradox we register rather than spin. The implications are (a) future moderator detection in this literature requires power calibrated to r ≈ .035 rather than r ≈ .07–.10, substantially raising the k threshold for adequately powered tests; and (b) future I-P meta-analyses should report bias-corrected pooled estimates as the primary inference. We register the paradox as a research-programme commitment — not as something a single article can resolve.")*

**Backup material:**
- P6 v1.3 §5.2 explicit "field-level paradox" paragraph
- Terrin et al. (2003) caveat: trim-and-fill over-corrects under high I²; we frame 53% as *upper bound* not point estimate
- Trim-and-fill imputed k = 58; Begg τ = −0.134 (p < .001); Egger borderline (p = .057)

**Trap to avoid:** Claiming P6's moderator nulls are *invalidated* by the publication-bias finding. They are *bounded* by it. The honest framing is the strongest framing.

## Tier 3 — Possible questions (have key numbers ready)

### Q3.1: "Singapore tại sao có turning point ~82% trong khi Vietnam ~40%?"

Brief answer: institutional regime difference; Singapore = advanced-economy ceiling (ICRV I, near-saturated digital), Vietnam = transitional (ICRV III/IV); curvature is shallower in Singapore consistent with CDCM prediction.

### Q3.2: "Three-level meta-analysis có over-fit không?"

Brief answer: REML estimator with k = 238 / K = 288 against 3 variance components is well-identified per Cheung (2014) sample-size guidance; we report compound-symmetric within-study covariance and independently verified via Python `verify_moderator_qm.py` cross-check.

### Q3.3: "Forest plot ICRV Frontier cell với K = 3 — bạn vẫn báo cáo nó?"

Brief answer: Yes, but framed honestly. The full-sample Q_M = 17.35 (p = .002) is driven by this small cell; drop-Frontier core-regime Q_M = 1.49 (p = .68). We report both and label the Frontier point estimate (r̄ = 0.349, K = 3) as an unreliable signal. Honest disclosure rather than concealment.

### Q3.4: "Manager characteristics (H4) — bạn xếp loại là kiểm soát chứ không phải moderator. Có nhất quán không?"

Brief answer: Yes, explicitly justified in Ch2 §2.5.3 methodological note. The label "H4" is retained for companion-paper compatibility but the methodological role is endogeneity control, not curvature moderation. Acknowledged that a formal reviewer could request renaming to "Control Block C1"; the dispute is documented and tracked for revision.

### Q3.5: "Vietnam Tier-1 DAI null vs Singapore Tier-1+2 DAI significant — có chắc là measurement boundary chứ không phải saturation?"

Brief answer: Both interpretations are consistent with the data; we present them as complementary, not alternative. P3 §5.1 explicitly: Tier-1 in Vietnam 2023 is approaching population-diffusion ceiling (49.8% website ownership) and may be proxy-obsolescent; P4 §3.2.3 R1 sensitivity diagnostic restricting Singapore DAI to Tier-1-only causes the quadratic interaction to weaken substantially (β from +3.119 to ~+1.55). The data are consistent with the joint claim: Tier-1 alone is insufficient for the conditional-scaling mechanism to be detectable.

### Q3.6: "OSF z37kn — bạn đã upload chưa?"

Brief answer (if uploaded by defense date): "Yes, the deposit at https://osf.io/z37kn contains the pre-registration, the analyzed corpus (k = 238 study database), the metafor R scripts, the Python verification script, the gold-standard evaluation report, the PRISMA 2020 flow diagram, and the OSF supplementary materials document." ## Tier 4 — Adversarial probes (be ready, but rare)

### Q4.1: "Affiliation đơn lẻ trên toàn portfolio — có conflict of interest không?"

Brief answer: All six papers (P3–P8) declare a single author affiliation — School of Economics, Can Tho University (CTU) — matching the doctoral institution at which this dissertation is defended. There is no commercial conflict of interest; the supervisor (PGS.TS. Phan Anh Tú) and candidate share the same institutional unit. Funding statements disclose that no specific grant supports the research, consistent with the Điều 27.1 ownership analysis in M-AIDA SHTT documentation.

### Q4.2: "Pisani et al. (2020) trong P3 §1.2 — kết quả của họ liên quan thế nào đến claim của bạn?"

Brief answer: Pisani et al. (2020 SMJ) showed cross-national pooled samples can artifactually inflate apparent inverted-U regularity that does not survive within-country analysis. P3's Vietnam evidence corroborates their concern: within Vietnam, the apparent inverted-U is best read as a step-function across the export-participation barrier (Melitz 2003 + Wagner 2007 + Bernard et al. 2007 mechanism), not as within-exporter saturation. We *qualify* rather than *overturn* their cross-national skepticism.

### Q4.3: "Bayesian meta-regression — bạn có cân nhắc không?"

Brief answer: Yes, and we discuss it as a future-research direction (P6 §5.4(b)). Frequentist three-level MARA is the standard reporting convention in the I-P meta-analytic literature (Marano et al. 2016 used REML two-level; we extend to three-level); Bayesian with informative priors would tighten Frontier-cell estimates with limited k, and we recommend it for the follow-up study with the Path B expansion corpus.

### Q4.4: "Bạn nói chương trình nghiên cứu — kế hoạch sau luận án là gì?"

Brief answer: Three immediate continuations: (a) Path B extraction completion → revised P6 at k ≈ 600–700 corpus to test the pre-registered moderators with adequate power; (b) Pacific SIDS extension using individual-participant-data meta-analysis if WBES Caribbean SIDS data becomes available, to test FIP generalizability beyond the Pacific sample; (c) Vietnamese policy-relevant follow-up using WBES 2026 wave (when released) plus administrative customs panel data to identify causal effects of the post-NDTP digital infrastructure rollout on Mekong Delta export productivity.
