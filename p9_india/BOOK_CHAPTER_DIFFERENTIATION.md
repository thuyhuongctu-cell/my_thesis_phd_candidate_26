# Differentiation Memo: P9' India Paper vs Book Chapter (Do & Phan 2025)

**Purpose**: Document tường minh sự khác biệt giữa book chapter đã xuất bản (Do & Phan, IntechOpen 2025) và paper P9' đang chuẩn bị để đảm bảo (a) không vi phạm self-plagiarism; (b) đóng góp mới rõ ràng cho editor và reviewer.

## Prior Work (đã xuất bản)

**Citation**:
> Do, T.H., & Phan, A.T. (2025). Internationalization and Firm Performance of Firms in India: The Role of Top Management. In *International Business Research – Traditional and Creative Approaches*. IntechOpen. DOI: [10.5772/intechopen.1011012](http://dx.doi.org/10.5772/intechopen.1011012)

**Key attributes:**
- **Dependent variable**: Return on Sales (ROS), mean 30.34%, range −660% to 98.166%
- **Independent variable**: Degree of Internationalization (DOI), linear, mean 0.056% (i.e., 5.6%), range 0–100%
- **Theoretical framework**: Uppsala model (Johanson & Vahlne 1977, 2017, 2020) + Upper Echelons Theory (Hambrick & Mason 1984)
- **Moderators**: Top manager's experience (years) + Top manager's gender (female dummy)
- **Estimation**: Feasible Generalized Least Squares (FGLS) on panel data
- **Sample**: N = 760 firm-year observations = 380 firms × 2 waves (matched panel subset of WBES India)
- **Findings**:
  - H1 confirmed: Internationalization NEGATIVE linear effect on firm performance (β = −36.66, p < 0.001)
  - H2 confirmed: Top manager experience positively moderates (β = +1.55, p < 0.001)
  - H3 confirmed: Female top management positively moderates (β = +77.03, p < 0.001)
- **Contribution claimed**: Extends Uppsala + Upper Echelons by integrating TMT characteristics in Indian developing-economy context

## New Work (P9' India, planned)

**Working title**:
> "Cross-Wave Stability of the Internationalisation–Performance Threshold in Indian Firms: Digital and Technological Capability Moderation Across a Decade of Institutional Transformation"

**Target journal**: Management International Review (Springer, ABS-3, Scopus Q1, IF 6.0+), OR, International Journal of Emerging Markets (IJOEM, Emerald, Scopus Q1)

**Key attributes:**
- **Dependent variable**: ln(Labour Productivity) = ln(Annual Sales / Number of Workers)
- **Independent variable**: Export Intensity (FSTS), QUADRATIC specification (FSTS + FSTS²)
- **Theoretical framework**: Conditional Digital Capability Moderation (CDCM), author's framework developed across portfolio (Author Citation P6 meta-analysis; P7 capstone)
- **Moderators**:
  - Technological Capability Index (TCI), z-standardised composite of certification, foreign technology, product innovation, R&D
  - Digital Adoption Index (DAI), Tier-1 (website binary, all waves) + Tier-2 (e-payment %, 2025 BREADY only)
- **Estimation**: OLS with HC1 robust standard errors + Lind-Mehlum U-test for inverted-U + Paternoster cross-wave z-test
- **Sample**: 3 full WBES India waves
  - 2014: N_raw = 9,281 (PICS3 schema)
  - 2022: N_raw = 9,376 (BEE intermediate)
  - 2025: N_raw = 10,479 (BREADY full)
  - Primary 2-wave (2014 vs 2025): pooled ~13,000-15,000 complete-core obs
  - 3-wave robustness: pooled ~20,000-22,000 complete-core obs
- **Research questions**:
  - RQ1: Is the I-P relationship inverted-U shaped in Indian firms (per-wave)?
  - RQ2: Is the turning point STRUCTURALLY DURABLE across 11 years of major institutional shocks (demonetisation 2016 + GST 2017 + IBC 2016 + UPI 2016 + PLI 2020 + COVID 2020-22 + Atmanirbhar Bharat 2020)?
  - RQ3: Do TCI and DAI moderate the threshold, and does the moderation pattern change between pre- and post-digital-infrastructure-saturation waves?
- **Identification innovation**: UPI rollout (April 2016) as **quasi-natural experiment** for DAI, 2014 = pre-UPI; 2025 = full UPI saturation (12B transactions/month)

## Self-Citation Strategy

P9' Introduction and Discussion sections will cite the book chapter as **prior author work**:

> "In earlier work, we examined the linear relationship between internationalisation and firm performance in a matched sub-sample of 380 Indian firms, with attention to top-management-team moderators (Do & Phan, 2025, IntechOpen DOI 10.5772/intechopen.1011012). The present paper takes a different methodological and theoretical approach: rather than assume a linear specification, we test the inverted-U threshold hypothesis common in the post-2000 internationalisation–performance literature (Lu & Beamish, 2004; Contractor, 2007; Marano et al., 2016) using the full WBES India samples across three waves, with attention to digital and technological capability moderation rather than top-management characteristics."

This formulation:
1. **Acknowledges** prior work explicitly (good academic practice + transparency)
2. **Differentiates** specification, theory, sample, and research question clearly
3. **Pre-empts** reviewer concern about overlap
4. **Cites the DOI** so editor can verify

## Cover Letter Disclosure

A formal disclosure paragraph will appear in the cover letter to the target journal editor:

> "We wish to disclose that we have previously published a related but distinct study on Indian firms in a book chapter (Do & Phan, 2025, IntechOpen, DOI 10.5772/intechopen.1011012). That chapter used a matched sub-sample of 380 firms, a linear DOI specification, and an Upper Echelons theoretical lens to test moderation by top-management characteristics. The present manuscript is fundamentally different in (i) theoretical framework (Conditional Digital Capability Moderation rather than Upper Echelons), (ii) empirical specification (quadratic inverted-U rather than linear), (iii) moderating constructs (technological/digital capability rather than TMT), (iv) sample (full WBES India 2014, 2022, and 2025 with ~10,000+ firms per wave rather than 380 matched firms), and (v) primary research question (threshold stability across institutional shocks rather than TMT moderation). We have included a self-citation in the literature review to acknowledge this prior work transparently. We confirm that the present manuscript is not under consideration elsewhere and that the empirical analysis reported here has not been published in whole or in substantive part."
