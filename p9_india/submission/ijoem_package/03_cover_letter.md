**Submission of Manuscript: "When Institutional Transformation Breaks the Threshold: The Disappearance of the Inverted-U Internationalisation–Performance Relationship in Indian Firms, 2014–2025"**

Dear Editor-in-Chief,

We are pleased to submit the above manuscript for consideration for publication in the *International Journal of Emerging Markets* (IJOEM). The manuscript presents an empirical study of the internationalisation–performance (I-P) threshold using three waves of World Bank Enterprise Survey microdata for India (2014 PICS3, N = 9,281; 2022 BEE, N = 9,376; 2025 BREADY, N = 10,479; pooled raw N = 29,136; pooled analytic N = 28,717). The 2014–2025 window spans India's unprecedented institutional and digital transformation decade: demonetisation 2016, Goods and Services Tax 2017, Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code 2016, Unified Payments Interface (UPI) launch 2016 and saturation 2024, Production-Linked Incentive scheme 2020, COVID-19 2020–2022, and the Atmanirbhar Bharat self-reliance policy 2020.

**Relevance to IJOEM**

The study contributes directly to IJOEM's core interest in firm-level dynamics in emerging markets. India is one of the largest and most institutionally complex emerging-market contexts for I-P research, and the three-wave design allows the study to test whether the curvilinear I-P relationship is structurally durable or dissolves across a decade of extreme institutional and digital transition, a question of direct relevance to IJOEM's readership. The manuscript also advances understanding of how state-provisioned digital public infrastructure (UPI) interacts with private capability accumulation in the emerging-market export production function, a frontier theme for the journal.

**Key Contributions**

First, this is the **first firm-level cross-wave threshold-stability test for India** using three full WBES waves. In 2014 the relationship is classically inverted-U with a turning point at FSTS = 61.8% (Lind–Mehlum joint p < 0.0001). By 2022 the inverted-U persists but the turning point migrates inward to 40.7%. By 2025 the curvature term is statistically indistinguishable from zero (β₂ = −0.16, p = 0.42) and the relationship becomes monotonically negative across the empirically relevant export-intensity range. Paternoster (1998) cross-wave z-tests decisively reject coefficient equality between 2014 and 2025 for both the linear (z = −7.94) and quadratic (z = +4.17) terms (both p < 0.0001). This identifies a previously untested **environmental scope condition** for the canonical Lu and Beamish (2004) inverted-U claim: the threshold is conditional on institutional stability and can be dissolved, not merely relocated, by sufficiently extreme institutional change.

Second, the manuscript **decomposes the Digital Adoption Index** into Tier-1 (private website binary, available all waves) and Tier-2 (e-payment percentage via UPI rails, 2025 BREADY only). The UPI Tier-2 interaction with export intensity is **negative** (FSTS × DAI_epay = −4.02, p = 0.004), refuting the developmental-state substitution hypothesis. We interpret this as a complementarity: domestic-rupee UPI infrastructure does not substitute for the cross-border financial infrastructure (SWIFT, correspondent banking, trade finance, export-credit insurance) that exporters specifically require.

Third, the manuscript documents a **context-dependent sign reversal** of the Technological Capability Index, a positive level-shifter of productivity in 2014 and 2022 that flips negative in 2025 with a positive export-intensity interaction, consistent with the strategic-emphasis trade-off under post-PLI/Atmanirbhar Bharat redirection.

**Prior Related Work, Transparent Disclosure**

We disclose that we have previously published a related but distinct study on Indian firms in a book chapter (Do & Phan, 2025, IntechOpen, DOI 10.5772/intechopen.1011012). That chapter used a matched sub-sample of 380 firms (760 firm-year observations), a linear DOI specification, FGLS panel estimation, and an Uppsala + Upper Echelons theoretical lens to test moderation by top-management characteristics. The present manuscript is fundamentally different in theoretical lens (the institutions-based view with Conditional Digital Capability Moderation), specification (quadratic inverted-U with Lind–Mehlum and Paternoster z-tests), moderators (TCI + DAI Tier-1 + DAI Tier-2), sample (full WBES across three waves, up to 10,479 firms per wave), and research question (threshold dynamics under institutional shift). We have included a transparent self-citation in the manuscript, and a full differentiation memo is available in the replication package. The empirical analysis reported here has not been published in whole or in substantive part.
