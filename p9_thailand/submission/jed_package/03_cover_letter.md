**Submission of Manuscript: "Cross-Wave Stability of the Internationalisation–Performance Relationship in Thai Firms: ASEAN Peer Evidence Across the COVID–RCEP Transition"**

Dear Editor-in-Chief,

We are pleased to submit the above manuscript for consideration for publication in the *Journal of Economics and Development* (JED). The paper presents an empirical study of the internationalisation–performance (I-P) relationship using two waves of World Bank Enterprise Survey microdata for Thailand (2016 pre-COVID/pre-RCEP, N = 705; 2025 post-COVID/post-RCEP, N = 766; pooled N = 1,471), positioned as Vietnam's closest ASEAN upper-middle peer.

**Fit with JED**

JED is a natural venue for this work for three reasons. First, **Thailand is an institutionally proximate peer to Vietnam** — both upper-middle ASEAN economies under RCEP framework — and the Thai-Vietnam comparative angle speaks directly to JED's readership of Vietnamese development economists, IB scholars, and ASEAN-focused policy analysts. Second, the paper completes a **multi-paper cross-Asian portfolio** (Vietnam, Singapore, China, Pacific SIDS, multi-country) of which JED is the natural home for the Vietnamese-ASEAN axis of this research programme. Third, the 2016–2025 window spans a uniquely consequential institutional transition (COVID-19 supply-chain disruption + RCEP entry into force Jan 2022 + Thailand BOI Investment Promotion Act 2017/2022), aligning with JED's editorial interest in policy-relevant evidence for sustainable economic development in transition economies.

**Key Contributions**

First, this is the **first firm-level cross-wave threshold-stability test for Thailand**. Prior Thai I-P studies (Pananond, 2008; Khanthavit, 2017) used single-wave or industry-specific samples, leaving the durability question unresolved.

Second, the 2016 vs 2025 comparison provides a **direct test of the cross-wave durability claim** documented for China (Author Citation, 2026 — IJOEM) and partly for Vietnam (Author Citation, 2026 — JED): does the upper-middle ASEAN context exhibit comparable threshold stability across major institutional shifts?

Third, the **Thailand-Vietnam ASEAN peer comparison** informs RCEP export-promotion policy harmonisation and the AEC 2025 Blueprint: cross-country I-P pattern similarity supports policy transfer between institutionally similar Asian peers.

**Policy Relevance**

The findings carry direct implications for Thailand's BOI BCG (Bio-Circular-Green) Economy Model 2021–2026 export-promotion programmes and for Vietnam-Thailand ASEAN cooperation under RCEP. The §5 policy discussion explicitly draws out implications for AEC 2025 implementation.
