## Bối cảnh tài chính Asia (ABM March 2026)

- **Emerging East Asia LCY bonds: USD30,6 nghìn tỷ** end-2025. Q4 2025 expansion 2,1% q-o-q (chậm hơn Q3 3,2%).
- **PRC chiếm 81,7%** regional bond stock; ASEAN aggregate USD2,7 nghìn tỷ (8,8% regional).
- **Government bonds USD19,9T; Corporate bonds USD10,0T**.
- **ASEAN+3 sustainable bonds: USD1,0 nghìn tỷ** (18,5% global), record USD275,9B issuance 2025. **Dẫn đầu thế giới (29,8% global sustainable issuance)**.
- **Bối cảnh thị trường Q4 2025–Q1 2026**: AI valuation concerns Nov 2025; geopolitical/trade tensions Jan 2026; better-than-expected Q4 GDP. Equity markets gained 4,2% market-weighted average; USD weakened 2,0% vs regional currencies.
- **Banks largest investor**: 35,7% LCY Treasury bonds; insurance/pension 29,2%; foreign 10,7%.

## Tái định vị Mongolia (ADB May 2026 Critical Minerals)

- **11 critical minerals** Mongolia có lợi thế: Cu, C (graphite), Li, Mo, Mn, Co, Fluorspar, Pt, Ni, W, REE.
- **Demand projections to 2050**: lithium 8-13×; manganese 18-29×; platinum 15,6-66,5×.
- **Supply shortfall 2040**: chỉ đủ 35% lithium, 59% Cu, 70% Co, 76% Ni từ existing/announced projects.
- **Mongolia copper exports 1,69 Mt (2024), $3,3B revenue, 22,1% tổng xuất khẩu**. Resources 61,4 Mt (90% concentrated in Oyu Tolgoi/Erdenet/Tsagaan Suvarga).
- **Oyu Tolgoi**: target 500.000 tons/năm copper 2028-2036 — major scale-up.
- **PRC processing dominance**: 100% refined natural graphite + dysprosium, 90% Mn, 70% Co, 60% Li, 40% Cu.
- **Indonesia nickel export ban 2020 → 40% global processing share by 2023** — successful policy precedent.
- **Korea-Mongolia Mineral Resources Cooperation Center** (2023) — bilateral framework.
- **SWOT Mongolia critical minerals**:
  - Strengths: vast reserves, proximity to PRC/JPN/KOR, government commitment, foreign investment in mining
  - Weaknesses: infrastructure constraints (sparse population, landlocked), human capital, regulatory uncertainty
  - Opportunities: rising global demand, new markets (EU, Japan, Korea), supply chain diversification, technological innovation
  - Threats: trade restrictions, competition (Australia/Canada), market volatility, ESG compliance costs

## AI in Finance Asia (Zhang, Qiu, Park & Tian, 2026)

- **249 peer-reviewed studies 1990-2025** (94 sources, AJG top-tier journals, 28 AI keywords + 48 finance terms).
- **Babina et al. (2024 — JFE)**: Firms adopting AI → higher growth + innovation intensity. **Direct support cho CĐ1 §4.4 DAI claim.**
- **PRC**: 80% bank risk models depend on dominant tech providers (Wang, Huang & Hong, 2024) → AI homogenization risk.
- **India**: AI in UPI giảm fraud 50% nhưng rural borrowers bị từ chối +24% (Anil & Misra, 2022) → "digital theatre" warning xác nhận.
- **Southeast Asia (Malaysia, Indonesia)**: AI fintech + Shariah-compliant nhưng regulatory fragmentation (Arsyad, Kharisma & Wiwoho, 2025).
- **5 risk dimensions**: (i) systemic risk transmission, (ii) micro-prudential resilience, (iii) regulatory technology, (iv) market structure evolution, (v) data-model-infrastructure dependence.
- Asia must reconceptualize AI as **"co-evolutionary governance substrate"** — not just regulatory target.

## Historical baseline integration (AEIR 2016)

- **2001 → 2015 intraregional trends**: trade 53→57%, FDI 47→53%, equity 12→20%, debt 8→18%, migration 42→37%, remittances 33→30%.
- **Asia FDI $527B (2015), up 9% YoY**. Outward Asian FDI declined 9,4% to $418B.
- **Special Theme: FDI drivers**: greenfield > M&A; GVC-FDI strongest in Asia; **institutional quality #1 driver** (especially M&A); business environment matters; RTAs help north-south; BITs with ISDM increase FDI 35,3%.
- **"Factory Asia"** still explains GVC-FDI; Japan + Korea = main sources.
- **Tourism receipts** Pacific = ~6% GDP; SIDS like Maldives, Palau, Vanuatu have >30% GDP from tourism — vulnerability.
